The Executive Team

Caitlin Dreisbach

Co-President

Cait Dreisbach received her Bachelor’s degree in biology from Cornell University and a Bachelor’s of Science in Nursing (BSN) from The Johns Hopkins University. Currently, she is in the dual degree Master’s in Data Science/PhD in Nursing program at the University of Virginia. As a labor and delivery nurse, her previous work has been focused on maternal risk factors for mental and physiological stress. With her additional training in data science, Cait is interested in combining machine learning and network models to better understand complex health conditions during pregnancy. Cait enjoys being outside, spending time with her rescue dog, Benji, iced coffee, and a good cheeseburger.

Zhongwen Luo

Co-President

Zhongwen Luo is currently a 4th year PhD candidate in Chemistry work with Prof. T. Brent Gunnoe in Chemistry and Robert J. Davis in Chemical Engineering to develop a catalytic process for Styrene monomer synthesis. Zhongwen started Data Science and Analytics Club with Cait to make it easier for UVA PhDs to learn data science skills. Zhongwen is particularly interested in data visualization and modeling in areas such as energy, sports analytics, healthcare and ethics. In his free time, he enjoy yoga, running and dancing.

William Adorno

VP for Finance

Will is currently pursuing a PhD in Systems and Information Engineering and has interests in statistical modeling, machine learning, multivariate statistics, design of experiments, computer vision, text analytics, decision support systems.

Charlotte McClintock

VP for Engagement

Charlotte McClintock is in her third year at the University of Virginia, starting an accelerated Master’s in Research Methods in Psychology this spring. Charlotte studies applied statistics & data science, human behavior, and politics/public policy. Charlotte currently working on an analysis of equity in provision of homelessness services, leading a team of volunteers on a project for the Colorado ACLU analyzing data from open records requests to inform bail reform policy in upcoming legislative session, and helping lead a team analyzing foster care data to investigate claims of racial disparity. In her free time, she loves reading books and long-form journalism (especially from Aeon), watching documentaries, sharing ideas, and solving problems.

Lisa Blair

Secretary

Lisa is a postdoctoral researcher at the School of Nursing with a clinical specialty certification in neonatal intensive care nursing. Her dissertation focused on genetic and social determinants of cognitive development in low birth weight children in a nationally-representative longitudinal cohort. Her current research interests focus on birth outcomes in rural Appalachia. When she’s not penning papers, writing grants, or running analyses, Lisa enjoys spending time with her husband and daughter, knitting, and writing fiction.
